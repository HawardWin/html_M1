Maheshwara : The God of Destruction

Maheshwara is a name for the Hindu God Shiva, meaning "Lord of the Universe."
It emphasizes Shiva's role as the supreme deity responsible for creation, preservation, and destruction, highlighting both his personal relationship with devotees and his cosmic significance.

Maheshwara : The God of Destruction

The Sanskrit name Maheśvara is composed of two "Mahā" and "Īśvara". The "ā" of mahā and the "ī" of īśvara combine to form a sandhi, which becomes "e", forming the word " Maheśvara. Mahā means "Great" and Īśvara means "God", hence the name Maheśvara means "The Great God".
